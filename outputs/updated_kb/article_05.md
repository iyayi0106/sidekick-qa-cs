# Article 5: Group Booking Minimums by Supplier Type

*Published: November 22, 2025 | Status: current*

Published: November 22, 2025
Category: Group Bookings > Getting Started
OVERVIEW
Not all travel qualifies as a "group booking." Each supplier type has minimum thresholds that determine when group
rates and contracts apply. This guide breaks down what counts as a group across different travel categories.
HOTELS
Minimum: 10+ rooms per night
This is the industry standard across most hotel brands and properties.
Examples:
— Wedding with 12 hotel rooms = Group 
— Family reunion with 8 rooms = Not a group (book as FIT)
— Corporate retreat with 25 rooms = Group 
Important notes:
— 10 rooms must be needed on the same night (peak night)
— Multi-night stays: Group minimum applies to peak occupancy night
— Some boutique properties may accept groups at 8+ rooms, but 10 is standard
Major chain minimums:
— Marriott: 10 rooms
— Hilton: 10 rooms
— Hyatt: 10 rooms
— IHG: 10 rooms
— Four Seasons: 10 rooms
— Most independents: 10 rooms
CRUISES
Minimum: 8+ staterooms (cabins)
Cruise lines measure groups by staterooms/cabins, not passengers.
Examples:
— 8 cabins with 16 passengers = Group 
— 6 cabins with 24 passengers (families of 4) = Not a group
— 10 cabins = Group 
Important notes:
— Some cruise lines use 8 staterooms, others use 10
— River cruises often have lower minimums (5-6 cabins)
— Luxury lines may have different thresholds
Common minimums by line type:
— Mass market (Carnival, Royal Caribbean, NCL): 8 staterooms
— Premium (Celebrity, Princess, Holland America): 8 staterooms
— Luxury (Regent, Oceania, Silversea): 5-8 staterooms
— River cruises: 5-6 staterooms
TOURS
Minimum: 10+ travelers
Tour operators and wholesalers typically require 10 travelers minimum for group pricing.
Examples:
— 12 travelers on custom Italy trip = Group 
— 8 travelers on African safari = Not a group (but ask—some operators are flexible)
— 15 travelers on group tour = Group 
Important notes:
— Some tour operators offer group rates starting at 8 travelers
— Custom/private tours may have different minimums
— Destination and season affect minimums
OTHER TRAVEL TYPES
DMCs (Destination Management Companies)
Typical minimum: 10-15 travelers
DMCs provide on-ground services (transfers, guides, activities). Minimums vary by destination and services
requested.
All-Inclusive Resorts
Minimum: Often 10+ rooms (same as hotels)
Some all-inclusive brands offer group coordinators or perks starting at 8 rooms.
Villas
Minimum: No standard group minimum
Villa bookings are usually priced per property, not per room. Large villa bookings (multiple villas for one group) may
qualify for group discounts.
Group Air
Minimum: Typically 10+ passengers
Airlines offer group rates starting around 10 passengers on the same flight. Group air has its own rules and
contracts.
WHAT HAPPENS BELOW MINIMUMS?
If your client doesn't meet group minimums:
Book as individual (FIT) reservations:
— Use standard booking processes
— Each room/cabin booked separately
— No group contract
— May still request room blocks without formal contract (courtesy hold)
Courtesy holds:
— Some hotels offer informal room blocks below 10 rooms
— No contract required
— Holds are not guaranteed
— Useful for small weddings (5-8 rooms)
COMBINATION SCENARIOS
Multi-property groups:
If your group is splitting across multiple hotels, each property is evaluated separately.
Example:
— 6 rooms at Hotel A
— 5 rooms at Hotel B
— Neither property reaches 10-room minimum
— Book as FIT at both
Multi-night varying occupancy:
Group minimum applies to peak night.
Example:
— Friday: 12 rooms (peak)
— Saturday: 8 rooms
— Sunday: 6 rooms
— This qualifies as a group (12 rooms on peak night)
WHY MINIMUMS MATTER
Below minimum:
— No group contract
— No group coordinator/support
— No TC credits or GAP points (cruises)
— No complimentary rooms
— Individual cancellation policies apply
At or above minimum:
— Group contract with negotiated terms
— Dedicated group coordinator
— Potential for amenities and upgrades
— Group cancellation and attrition terms
— Commission on full group
EDGE CASES AND EXCEPTIONS
Ask the supplier when:
— Group is 1-2 rooms/cabins below minimum
— High-value group (luxury, extended stay)
— Repeat client or loyal advisor
— Shoulder season or low-demand period
Some suppliers will make exceptions, especially if the group has potential for growth or represents significant
revenue.
