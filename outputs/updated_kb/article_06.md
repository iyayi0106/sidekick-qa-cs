# Article 6: Group Payment Collection Process

*Published: August 12, 2024 | Status: current*

Published: August 12, 2024
Category: Group Bookings > Operations
OVERVIEW
Managing payments for group bookings requires coordination between you, your client (the group organizer), and
individual travelers. This guide covers best practices for collecting and processing group payments.
PAYMENT STRUCTURE
Most group bookings follow a tiered payment schedule:
Hotels
Initial deposit: 20-30% at contract signing
Second payment: 60 days before arrival
Final payment: 30 days before arrival
Cruises
Initial deposit: $250-500 per person at booking
Final payment: 120 days before sailing (for most sailings over 6 nights)
Shorter cruises may have different timelines:
— 2-5 night cruises: 75 days before sailing
— 6+ night cruises: 120 days before sailing
Tours
Initial deposit: 25-30% at booking
Second payment: 90 days before departure
Final payment: 60 days before departure
TWO COLLECTION MODELS
Model 1: Advisor Collects from Travelers
You collect payments directly from each traveler and submit group payments to the supplier.
Pros:
— Full control over payment timing
— Direct relationship with travelers
— Can track who has/hasn't paid
Cons:
— Time-intensive
— You're responsible if travelers don't pay
— Need merchant account for credit card processing
Best for: Small groups (10-20 travelers), advisors with established payment processing
Model 2: Group Organizer Collects Payments
The group organizer (client) collects money from travelers and sends you lump sum payments.
Pros:
— Less administrative work for you
— Group organizer is responsible for collecting
— Only deal with one point of contact
Cons:
— Less visibility into who has paid
— Dependent on organizer's follow-through
— May be delays if organizer is slow to collect
Best for: Larger groups, corporate groups, weddings where couple handles guests
RECOMMENDED TIMELINE (HOTELS)
12+ months before event:
— Contract signed
— Initial deposit due (20-30%)
6 months before:
— Begin collecting traveler deposits
— Rooming list takes shape
90 days before:
— Second deposit due to hotel (if applicable)
60 days before:
— Push for final traveler commitments
— Cut-off date approaching
45 days before:
— Cut-off date (release unused rooms)
— Final payment due to hotel
30 days before:
— Finalize rooming list
— Submit special requests
RECOMMENDED TIMELINE (CRUISES)
12+ months before sailing:
— Book group (contracted or spec)
— Collect initial deposits ($250-500/person)
6 months before:
— Promote sailing
— Continue collecting bookings
120 days before (final payment):
— Collect final payments from travelers
— Submit to cruise line
90 days before:
— Finalize cabin assignments
— Submit dietary restrictions, special requests
60 days before:
— Work with group coordinator on TC Credits and GAP Points
PAYMENT METHODS
Credit Cards
Pros: Instant payment, secure, traveler protection
Cons: Processing fees (2-3%), may hit credit limits on large groups
Best for: Individual traveler payments
ACH/Bank Transfer
Pros: Lower fees, good for large payments
Cons: Slower processing (3-5 days), requires bank account info
Best for: Group organizer lump sum payments, final payments
Checks
Pros: No processing fees
Cons: Slow, risk of bounced checks, outdated
Best for: Older clients, smaller deposits
Wire Transfer
Pros: Fast, secure, good for international
Cons: High fees ($25-50), requires bank info
Best for: International groups, very large payments
HANDLING LATE PAYMENTS
Set internal deadlines earlier than supplier deadlines
If hotel wants final payment 30 days out, tell clients it's due 40 days out. This gives you buffer.
Send reminder emails at key milestones:
— 60 days before: "Second payment due in 2 weeks"
— 45 days before: "Final payment due soon"
— 35 days before: "Final reminder—payment due in 5 days"
Have a plan for non-payment:
— Can you cover shortfall temporarily?
— Will you charge late fees?
— At what point do you cancel the traveler's reservation?
REFUND AND CANCELLATION POLICIES
Hotels
Cancellation penalties vary by contract. Typical structure:
— 90+ days out: Refund minus deposit
— 60-90 days: 50% penalty
— 30-60 days: 75% penalty
— 0-30 days: No refund
Always communicate cancellation terms clearly when collecting payments.
Cruises
Cruise lines have specific cancellation schedules based on sailing length and departure date. Generally:
— 120+ days out: Refund minus deposit ($100-250/person)
— 90-119 days: 50% penalty
— 60-89 days: 75% penalty
— 0-59 days: 100% penalty (no refund)
Important: Cruise cancellation policies are stricter than hotel policies.
MANAGING GROUP FINANCES
Use a spreadsheet to track:
— Traveler names
— Payment amounts and dates
— Balance owed
— Cancellation requests
Separate business and personal finances:
— Use a dedicated business account for group payments
— Don't mingle group funds with personal money
Track supplier payment deadlines:
— Set reminders for deposit and final payment dates
— Don't miss deadlines (penalties can apply)
DEPOSITS VS. FINAL PAYMENTS
Deposits:
— Typically non-refundable or partially refundable
— Lock in rates and hold inventory
— Usually 20-30% of total
Final payments:
— Due 30-120 days before travel (depending on supplier)
— Remaining balance after deposits
— Often non-refundable once submitted
Make sure travelers understand the difference. Many assume deposits are fully refundable.
PAYMENT DISPUTES
What to do if a traveler disputes a charge:
1. Review your payment terms (did you communicate policies clearly?)
2. Check the supplier's cancellation policy
3. Try to work out a compromise (credit toward future travel?)
4. Escalate to Booking Platform team if needed
Protect yourself:
— Send written confirmation of all payments
— Keep records of cancellation policy acknowledgments
— Use contracts for larger groups

RED FLAGS
Watch out for:
— Group organizer who's slow to collect payments (puts you at risk)
— Travelers asking to pay "later" repeatedly
— Last-minute cancellations after final payment deadline
— Requests to bypass payment terms
If payments aren't coming in on schedule, escalate with the group organizer early.
WHEN TO ESCALATE TO BOOKING PLATFORM
Contact BP team for:
— Complex payment disputes
— International payment questions
— Large group payments (50+ travelers)
— Supplier payment issues
KEY TAKEAWAYS
— Always set internal deadlines earlier than supplier deadlines
— Choose collection model based on group size and organizer reliability
— Track payments carefully with spreadsheets or tools
— Communicate cancellation policies clearly upfront
— Use secure payment methods (credit cards, ACH)
