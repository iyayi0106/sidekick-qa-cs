WHAT QUALIFIES AS A GROUP?
Group travel is when you're booking for multiple people traveling together. Here's when bookings qualify for group
rates:
Hotels: 10+ rooms
Cruises: 8+ staterooms
Tours: 10+ travelers
Once you reach these minimums, you'll work with the supplier's group sales team instead of booking individual
reservations.
WHY BOOK GROUPS?
For you:
— Higher commission on total group value
— Easier to manage one contract vs. many individual bookings
— Build expertise in a lucrative niche
For clients:
— Better rates
— Rooms/cabins together
— Special amenities
— Dedicated support
TYPES OF GROUPS
Leisure Groups
— Weddings
— Family reunions
— Milestone birthdays
— Travel clubs
Corporate Groups
— Meetings and conferences
— Incentive trips
— Team retreats
BASIC GROUP PROCESS
1. Inquiry: Client reaches out about group travel
2. Gather details: Size, dates, budget, purpose
3. Request quotes: Contact suppliers for group proposals
4. Present options: Review with client
5. Contract: Sign agreement with selected supplier
6. Deposit: Submit initial payment
7. Manage bookings: Collect traveler info and preferences
8. Finalize: Submit rooming list by cut-off date
9. Support: Coordinate details before and during travel
KEY TERMS TO KNOW
Room Block: Number of rooms reserved for your group
Attrition: Minimum rooms you must fill (typically 15-20% below block size)
Cut-off Date: Deadline to release unused rooms
Run of House: Hotel assigns room types based on availability
Rooming List: Document with traveler names, room preferences, special requests
GETTING STARTED TIPS
1. Start small: Begin with groups of 10-15 people
2. Use templates: Fora provides group intake forms and contract templates
3. Build relationships: Connect with hotel and cruise line group sales teams
4. Set expectations: Be clear with clients about deadlines and deposits
COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
— Not reading the contract carefully
— Missing cut-off dates
— Underestimating group size
— Forgetting to factor in attrition risk
— Not explaining cancellation policies to clients
WHEN TO ESCALATE
Reach out to Booking Platform team for:
— Groups over 50 rooms/cabins
— Complex contract terms
— Multi-property groups
— International destinations you're unfamiliar with
