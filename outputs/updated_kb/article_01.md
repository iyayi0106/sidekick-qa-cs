WHAT IS A GROUP BOOKING?
A group booking is when you're arranging travel for multiple people traveling together. At Fora, we define groups
by the number of rooms, cabins, or travelers depending on the travel type:
— Hotels: 10+ rooms
— Cruises: 8+ staterooms
— Tours: 10+ travelers
Group bookings follow different processes than individual (FIT) bookings and typically involve contracts, deposits,
and special terms.
WHY BOOK GROUPS?
Benefits for advisors:
— Higher overall commission (commission on entire group vs. individual bookings)
— Simplified logistics (one contract vs. multiple individual reservations)
— Stronger client relationships (handling complex needs)
— Economies of scale
Benefits for clients:
— Group rates (often lower than individual rates)
— Room/cabin blocks held together
— Customized experiences
— Dedicated support from suppliers
IMPORTANT DIFFERENCES FROM FIT BOOKINGS
What changes with group bookings:
1. No preferred partner perks: Group bookings do not offer preferred partner perks and amenities
2. Contract required: Most groups require a formal contract with terms
3. Deposits and attrition: Groups typically require upfront deposits and have attrition clauses
4. Cut-off dates: Room blocks must be released by a specific date
5. Commission structure: May differ from FIT rates
TYPES OF GROUP BOOKINGS
Hotels
Minimum: 10+ rooms
Common group types:
— Weddings
— Corporate meetings
— Reunions
— Incentive travel
Key terms:
— Run of House (ROH): Group receives room types based on availability
— Room block: Specific number of rooms held for the group
— Attrition: Minimum number of rooms that must be filled (typically 20-30% below block size)
— Cut-off date: Deadline to release unused rooms (typically 30-60 days before arrival)
Cruises
Minimum: 8+ staterooms
Booking types:
— Speculative: Flexible hold, no deposit required initially
— Contracted: Formal commitment with deposit
— Charter: Full or partial ship buyout
Cruise-specific benefits:
— Tour Conductor (TC) Credits: Earned based on group size, typically 1 per 8-10 cabins
— Group Amenity Points (GAP): Can be exchanged for onboard credit, events, amenities
Tours
Minimum: 10+ travelers
Common scenarios:
— Custom group itineraries
— Special interest groups
— Educational travel
— Milestone celebrations
COMMISSION STRUCTURE
Group bookings typically earn commission based on total group revenue. Standard rates:
— Hotels (major chains): 7%
— Hotels (boutique/luxury): 10-12%
— Cruises: ~16%
— Tour operators: 16-18%
— Wholesalers: ~15%
Note: Rates may vary by supplier and group size.
KEY CONTRACT TERMS
Attrition
The minimum number of rooms/cabins the group must fill. Industry standard is 20-30% attrition (meaning the
group must fill 70-80% of the block).
Example:
— Block of 50 rooms
— 20% attrition
— Group must fill at least 40 rooms or pay penalty
Cut-off Date
The date by which the group must finalize their room count. Unused rooms are released back to the hotel. Typically
30-60 days before arrival.
Deposit Schedule
Groups usually require deposits at booking and additional payments at intervals before arrival.
Complimentary Rooms
Hotels often provide complimentary rooms based on group size. Standard ratio is 1:40 (one comp room per 40
paid rooms).
GROUP BOOKING PROCESS
1. Initial inquiry: Gather group details (size, dates, budget, purpose)
2. RFP (Request for Proposal): Submit formal request to suppliers
3. Review proposals: Compare rates, terms, amenities
4. Contract negotiation: Work with supplier on terms
5. Contract signature: Formalize agreement
6. Deposit payment: Submit initial deposit
7. Manage rooming list: Collect traveler details and preferences
8. Cut-off date management: Finalize room count before deadline
9. Final payment: Submit remaining balance
10. Pre-arrival support: Coordinate details with supplier
11. Post-trip follow-up: Gather feedback, handle issues
WHEN TO INVOLVE FORA TEAMS
— Booking Platform team: Contract review, supplier negotiations, unusual requests
— Tour Ops team: Destination-specific guidance (especially Japan, Africa), custom itineraries
— Support team: Escalations, client issues during travel

WHAT THIS GUIDE DOESN'T COVER
This guide provides foundational knowledge for group bookings at Fora. For detailed operational guidance on
specific scenarios, contract negotiations, or supplier-specific policies, consult with Booking Platform or Tour Ops
teams.
## Operational addendum (HQ sources, Jan 2026)

**Hyatt group program:** Standard properties 10% commission (up from 7%); luxury collection 12%; +1% bonus for 25+ room groups. Wedding/corporate blocks: 25% attrition; social groups: 30%. See HQ Source 3.

**TC credits:** Credits may be split across redemption types (e.g., one for group event, two converted to commission ~$800–1200 each). Confirm allocation 60–90 days before sailing with cruise line coordinator. See HQ Source 2.

**Wedding comp ratios:** 1:40 is standard but negotiable (often 1:30 at luxury properties). With 35 rooms at 1:30, one comp room is achievable. See HQ Source 1.
