# Article 8: TC Credits and GAP Points: What Advisors Need to Know

*Published: October 15, 2025 | Status: current*

Published: October 15, 2025
Category: Group Bookings > Cruises
OVERVIEW
Cruise groups earn special perks beyond standard commission: Tour Conductor (TC) Credits and Group Amenity
Points (GAP). Understanding how these work helps you maximize value for your clients and yourself.
TOUR CONDUCTOR (TC) CREDITS
What are TC Credits?
TC Credits are earned based on your group size. Think of them as rewards from the cruise line for bringing group
business.
How many do you earn?
Earning rate: Approximately 1 TC credit per 8-10 cabins booked
The exact rate varies by cruise line:
— Mass market lines (Carnival, Royal Caribbean, NCL): 1 per 8 cabins
— Premium lines (Celebrity, Princess): 1 per 8-10 cabins
— Luxury lines (Regent, Oceania, Silversea): 1 per 5-8 cabins
Example:
— 20-cabin group on Royal Caribbean = 2-3 TC credits
— 40-cabin group on Celebrity = 4-5 TC credits
How can TC Credits be used?
TC Credits can be redeemed for:
1. Free cabin for group leader/tour conductor
— Most common use
— Group organizer travels free
2. Onboard events or amenities
— Private cocktail party
— Specialty dining for the group
— Shore excursions
— Onboard credit distributed to travelers
3. Additional commission
— Convert TC credit to extra advisor commission
— Amount varies by cruise line
Important notes:
— TC Credits must be used on the same sailing (cannot be banked for future cruises)
— One TC credit typically equals one free inside cabin; upgrades available for additional cost
— Credits are awarded when group meets minimum cabin threshold
— Advisor decides how to allocate TC credits
GROUP AMENITY POINTS (GAP)
What are GAP Points?
GAP Points are another cruise line perk awarded to groups. These points can be exchanged for various amenities
to enhance the group experience.
How many do you earn?
GAP Points are awarded based on total cabins booked. The formula varies by cruise line.
Typical structure:
— 10 cabins = 50 GAP points
— 20 cabins = 120 GAP points
— 40 cabins = 300 GAP points
Larger groups earn points at a better rate (economies of scale).
What can GAP Points be redeemed for?
Onboard credit (OBC):
— Applied to travelers' shipboard accounts
— Can be split among group or given to specific cabins
Welcome amenities:
— Champagne, chocolate-covered strawberries, or welcome gifts in cabins
Group events:
— Happy hours
— Private parties
— Themed events
Wi-Fi packages:
— Internet access for group members
Specialty dining:
— Group dinners at specialty restaurants
Shore excursions:
— Discounted or complimentary group excursions
Beverage packages or spa services:
— Some cruise lines allow GAP redemption for these
How to use GAP Points:
Work with the cruise line's group coordinator to select amenities. Redemption happens before sailing (typically
30-60 days out).
TC CREDITS VS. GAP POINTS: WHAT'S THE DIFFERENCE?
| Feature | TC Credits | GAP Points |
|---------|------------|------------|
| Earned based on | Cabins booked | Cabins booked |
| Redemption | Free cabin, commission, or event | Amenities, OBC, events |
| Flexibility | Limited redemption options | Many redemption options |
| Who benefits | Group leader or advisor | Entire group |
| Typical use | Free cabin for organizer | OBC or welcome gifts for travelers |
STRATEGIC USE OF TC CREDITS AND GAP POINTS
Scenario 1: Affinity Group (Travel Club)
— TC Credits: Use for free cabin for club president
— GAP Points: Distribute as onboard credit to all members ($50 per cabin)
Scenario 2: Incentive Trip (Corporate)
— TC Credits: Convert to additional commission (company paid for all travel)
— GAP Points: Private cocktail party and specialty dining event
Scenario 3: Family Reunion
— TC Credits: Free cabin for family organizer (grandparents)
— GAP Points: Welcome amenities in all cabins + group shore excursion
Scenario 4: Milestone Celebration (50th Birthday)
— TC Credits: Free cabin for birthday honoree
— GAP Points: Private party, OBC for guests, champagne in cabins
WHEN ARE TC CREDITS AND GAP POINTS AWARDED?
— Contracted groups: Awarded once group meets minimum cabin commitment
— Speculative groups: Awarded as cabins are booked (may increase as group grows)
— Final calculation: Determined at final payment (90 days before sailing)
If your group shrinks below minimum after final payment, TC Credits and GAP may be reduced.
WORKING WITH CRUISE LINE GROUP COORDINATORS
Your group coordinator will help you:
— Calculate TC Credits and GAP Points
— Choose redemption options
— Coordinate onboard events
— Manage changes to group size
Contact your coordinator 60-90 days before sailing to finalize TC and GAP usage.
COMMON QUESTIONS
Can I split TC Credits?
No. Each TC credit equals one cabin. You cannot split a credit across multiple cabins.
Can I save GAP Points for another cruise?
No. TC Credits and GAP Points must be used on the sailing for which they were earned.
What if my group grows after I've redeemed GAP Points?
Contact your group coordinator. Additional points may be awarded, and you can adjust redemptions.
Do river cruises offer TC Credits and GAP?
Some do, but terms vary widely. Check with each river cruise line.
IMPORTANT REMINDERS
— Always factor TC Credits and GAP into your group value proposition
— Explain these benefits to clients when pitching group travel
— Work closely with cruise line group coordinators to maximize value
— Redemption deadlines vary by cruise line—don't miss them

### TC credit splitting (HQ Source 2)

Advisors may allocate TC credits across redemption types on the same sailing—for example, one credit for a group event (~$800 value) and two converted to additional commission (~$800–1200 each, varies by line). Confirm final cabin count before committing; redemption forms due 60–90 days pre-sailing.
