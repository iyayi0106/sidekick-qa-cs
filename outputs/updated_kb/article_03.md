# Article 3: Understanding Hotel Group Contracts

*Published: January 8, 2026 | Status: current*

Published: January 8, 2026
Category: Group Bookings > Hotels
OVERVIEW
When booking 10+ hotel rooms, you'll typically work with the hotel's group sales team to negotiate a contract. This
guide covers the key terms you'll encounter and what to look for during contract review.
ESSENTIAL CONTRACT TERMS
Run of House (ROH)
The hotel assigns room types based on availability at check-in. Your group doesn't get to choose specific room
categories, but the hotel ensures appropriate accommodations.
When to accept ROH:
— Flexible group with mixed preferences
— Budget-conscious clients
— Lower rates offered in exchange
When to negotiate specific room types:
— Wedding blocks (consistency matters)
— VIP/executive groups
— Groups with accessibility needs
Attrition Clause
The minimum percentage of your room block that must be filled. If the group doesn't meet this threshold, you may
owe payment for unfilled rooms.
Standard attrition: 20-30% (meaning group must fill 70-80% of block)
Example:
— 40-room block
— 25% attrition
— Group must fill at least 30 rooms
— If only 28 rooms fill, client pays for 2 additional rooms
Negotiation tip: Higher attrition protects the client but may result in higher rates. Lower attrition gets better pricing
but increases risk.
Cut-off Date
The deadline to finalize your rooming list and release unused rooms back to the hotel. Typically 30-60 days before
arrival.
After cut-off:
— Unused rooms are released
— Group is charged for rooms up to attrition minimum
— Hotel can resell released inventory
Pro tip: Set internal cut-off 7-10 days before hotel deadline to give yourself buffer.
Deposit Schedule
Most hotel group contracts require:
1. Initial deposit: 10-25% at contract signing
2. Second deposit: 30-60 days before arrival
3. Final payment: At cut-off date or 14-30 days before arrival
Deposit amounts and timing are negotiable based on group size and hotel policy.
Complimentary Rooms
Hotels provide free rooms based on group size. Standard ratio: 1:40 (one comp room per 40 paid rooms).
Example:
— 80 paid rooms = 2 comp rooms
— 45 paid rooms = 1 comp room
— 35 paid rooms = 0 comp rooms
Comp rooms can be used for group organizer, tour manager, or sold to reduce client costs.
Sliding Scale Cancellation Policy
Cancellation penalties increase as arrival date approaches.
Typical structure:
— 90+ days out: Full refund minus deposit
— 60-89 days: 25% penalty
— 30-59 days: 50% penalty
— 0-29 days: 100% penalty (full payment due)
Always review cancellation terms carefully and explain them clearly to clients.
NEGOTIABLE ITEMS
What you can typically negotiate:
Rates:
— Base room rate
— Discounts for larger groups
— Shoulder date flexibility (slightly different dates for better rates)
Amenities:
— Complimentary breakfast
— Welcome reception
— Room upgrades
— Late checkout
— Resort fee waivers
Terms:
— Attrition percentage
— Cut-off date extension
— Deposit schedule adjustments
— Flexible cancellation terms
Meeting Space:
— Complimentary meeting rooms
— AV equipment
— Wi-Fi access
WHAT'S USUALLY NON-NEGOTIABLE
— Service charges and taxes
— Parking fees (sometimes)
— Resort fees at resort properties
— Minimum length of stay during peak periods
CONTRACT REVIEW CHECKLIST
Before signing, verify:
— [ ] Room block size matches your needs
— [ ] Rates are competitive and locked in
— [ ] Attrition clause is reasonable (20-30% range)
— [ ] Cut-off date gives adequate time
— [ ] Deposit schedule is manageable for client
— [ ] Cancellation policy is clear
— [ ] Complimentary room ratio is included (1:40)
— [ ] Any promised amenities are in writing
— [ ] Force majeure clause protects against unforeseen events
— [ ] Walking policy is defined (what happens if hotel oversells)
RED FLAGS
Watch out for:
— Attrition below 15% or above 35%: Too risky or too expensive
— Cut-off date less than 30 days: Not enough time to fill block
— No force majeure clause: Group at risk if event cancelled for legitimate reasons
— Unclear walking policy: What if hotel can't accommodate your group?
— Service charges not itemized: Hidden costs can surprise clients
WHEN TO ESCALATE TO BOOKING PLATFORM
Reach out to BP team for:
— Unfavorable contract terms you can't negotiate
— Multi-property or multi-destination groups
— Groups over 100 rooms
— International group bookings
— Complex F&B minimums or meeting space needs
— Legal questions about contract language
