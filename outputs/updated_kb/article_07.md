# Article 7: Cruise Group Booking Types Explained

*Published: December 18, 2025 | Status: current*

Published: December 18, 2025
Category: Group Bookings > Cruises
OVERVIEW
Cruise group bookings work differently than hotel groups. There are three main booking types, each with different
requirements, flexibility, and benefits. Understanding when to use each type is key to serving your clients
effectively.
GROUP MINIMUMS
Minimum for cruise groups: 8+ staterooms
Group sizes:
— Small groups: 8-15 staterooms
— Medium groups: 15-50 staterooms
— Large groups: 50-250+ staterooms
— Buyouts/Charters: Full or partial ship
THREE BOOKING TYPES
1. Speculative Hold (Spec)
A non-committal hold on cabins while you gauge interest from your group.
Key features:
— No initial deposit required
— Flexible hold on inventory
— Can release cabins without penalty
— Recall date: Cruise line can recall cabins with notice (typically 30-90 days before sailing)
Best for:
— Testing interest in a sailing
— Groups still forming
— Uncertain final headcount
— New advisors building group travel business
How it works:
1. Request spec hold from cruise line group desk
2. Promote sailing to your client base
3. As individuals commit, book them into the block
4. Before recall date, either convert to contracted or release
Important: Spec holds give cruise line flexibility to recall inventory if the ship starts filling. Don't wait too long to
convert.
2. Contracted Group
A formal commitment with deposit and minimum cabin requirements.
Key features:
— Deposit required (typically 10-20% of total)
— Minimum cabin commitment (varies by cruise line)
— Attrition clause (similar to hotel groups)
— Better pricing and amenities than spec
— Locked-in rates
Best for:
— Committed groups with confirmed travelers
— Larger groups (15+ cabins)
— Peak sailing dates
— Groups wanting best rates and perks
How it works:
1. Sign group contract with cruise line
2. Submit initial deposit
3. Promote sailing and collect traveler deposits
4. Meet minimum cabin requirement by cut-off date
5. Pay for unfilled cabins if below attrition threshold
Example:
— Contract for 20 cabins
— 20% attrition (must fill 16 cabins)
— If only 14 cabins fill, group pays for 2 additional cabins
3. Charter (Full or Partial)
Exclusive use of an entire ship or large section.
Key features:
— Buyout of ship or significant portion (100+ cabins)
— Custom itineraries possible
— Exclusive events and branding
— Significant upfront commitment
— Highest level of customization
Best for:
— Large corporate incentive groups
— Destination weddings with 100+ guests
— Conferences or conventions
— Ultra-luxury private experiences
How it works:
1. Submit charter request to cruise line
2. Negotiate custom terms (itinerary, pricing, F&B, entertainment)
3. Substantial deposit (often 25%+ of total)
4. Work with cruise line event planning team
5. Coordinate all logistics directly
CHOOSING THE RIGHT BOOKING TYPE
| Scenario | Recommended Type |
|----------|------------------|
| Testing interest for a future sailing | Speculative |
| 8-12 cabins, casual group | Speculative |
| 15+ cabins, committed travelers | Contracted |
| Peak season sailing (limited inventory) | Contracted |
| Want best pricing and amenities | Contracted |
| 100+ cabins, exclusive experience | Charter |
| Custom itinerary needed | Charter |
CONVERTING FROM SPEC TO CONTRACTED
Many groups start as spec and convert to contracted once interest is confirmed.
When to convert:
— Group size justifies better contracted rates
— Recall date approaching
— You've reached 60-70% of target cabin count
— Sailing is filling and you want to lock inventory
Process:
1. Contact cruise line group desk
2. Negotiate contracted terms
3. Submit deposit
4. Existing spec bookings transfer to contracted block
GROUP BENEFITS: TC CREDITS AND GAP POINTS
Regardless of booking type, groups earn special perks:
Tour Conductor (TC) Credits:
— Earned at rate of approximately 1 TC credit per 8-10 cabins (varies by cruise line)
— Can be redeemed for: free cabin for group leader, onboard events, or additional commission
Group Amenity Points (GAP):
— Points awarded based on group size
— Redeemable for: onboard credit, welcome gifts, happy hours, specialty dining, shore excursions, Wi-Fi
packages
TC Credits and GAP details vary by cruise line. Check with each line's group desk for specifics.
IMPORTANT NOTES
Cabins vs. Berths:
— Cruise lines measure groups in staterooms/cabins, not passengers
— A cabin with 2 passengers = 1 cabin toward group minimum
— A cabin with 4 passengers = still 1 cabin (not 2)
Recall Dates:
— Spec holds have recall dates when cruise line can take back inventory
— Contracted groups also have final payment and cut-off dates
— Missing these dates can result in lost inventory or penalties
Individual Names:
— Spec bookings: Log individually as travelers commit
— Contracted: May hold cabins without names initially, add names before final payment
