OVERVIEW
Group bookings earn commission based on the total value of the group. Understanding commission structures
helps you price appropriately and set client expectations.
HOTEL GROUP COMMISSIONS
Major chains (Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt, IHG): 8-10%
Boutique and luxury hotels: 10-15%
All-inclusive resorts: 10-12%
How hotel commission is calculated:
Commission is typically calculated on:
— Room rate × number of room nights
— Does NOT include: taxes, resort fees, parking, F&B
Example:
— 20 rooms × 3 nights = 60 room nights
— Average rate: $200/night
— Total room revenue: $12,000
— Commission at 10%: $1,200
When is commission paid?
Most hotels pay commission 30-60 days after group checkout.
CRUISE GROUP COMMISSIONS
Standard commission: 15-16%
Luxury cruise lines: 18-20%
River cruises: 15-18%
How cruise commission is calculated:
Commission is calculated on:
— Base fare (per person)
— Port charges and taxes (sometimes included, sometimes not—check with cruise line)
— Does NOT include: Gratuities, shore excursions, specialty dining, beverages
Example:
— 20 cabins × 2 passengers = 40 passengers
— Average fare: $2,000/person
— Total cruise revenue: $80,000
— Commission at 16%: $12,800
Tiered commission structures:
Some cruise lines offer higher commission for larger groups:
— 8-15 cabins: 16%
— 16-30 cabins: 17%
— 31+ cabins: 18%
When is commission paid?
Cruise lines typically pay commission after final payment (90 days before sailing) or after sailing, depending on the
line.
TOUR OPERATOR COMMISSIONS
Standard tour operators: 15-18%
Luxury/custom tours: 18-20%
Adventure tour operators: 10-15%
Commission is calculated on the land package price (accommodations, transportation, activities). Flights are
usually booked separately.
DMC (DESTINATION MANAGEMENT COMPANY) COMMISSIONS
Typical commission: 10-15%
DMCs provide on-ground services. Commission varies based on:
— Destination
— Services provided
— Volume of business
WHOLESALER COMMISSIONS
Typical commission: 10-12%
Wholesalers package hotels + tours + transfers. Commission is lower because they're aggregating services.
GROUP AIR COMMISSIONS
Group air bookings typically earn minimal commission from airlines (if any). Instead:
— Charge a service fee to the client
— Work with consolidators who offer net rates + markup
Most advisors charge $50-100 per ticket for group air coordination.
SPECIAL PROGRAMS AND OVERRIDES
Many suppliers offer bonus commission for high-performing advisors:
Virtuoso groups: May earn additional commission or amenities
Preferred suppliers: Often offer 1-2% commission override
Volume bonuses: Book 100+ rooms/year with a chain  earn extra commission
COMMISSION PAYMENT TIMELINES
| Supplier Type | Typical Payment Timing |
|---------------|------------------------|
| Hotels | 30-60 days after checkout |
| Cruises | 90 days pre-sailing or post-sailing |
| Tour operators | 30-45 days after trip start |
| DMCs | 30 days after services rendered |
| All-inclusives | 60-90 days after checkout |
PROTECTING YOUR COMMISSION
Always:
— Register the group with the supplier
— Get commission agreement in writing
— Track group bookings in your CRM
— Follow up if payment is late
Watch out for:
— Clients booking direct after you've done the work (send confirmation emails establishing your role)
— Suppliers claiming group didn't meet minimum (document final numbers)
— Commission clawbacks if group cancels (understand cancellation commission policies)
COMMISSION VS. SERVICE FEES
Some advisors charge clients a service fee on top of commission for:
— Complex multi-destination groups
— Groups requiring significant coordination
— Groups with custom requests
Typical service fees: $50-150 per person or 5-10% of total trip cost
IMPORTANT NOTES
— Commission structures can vary by season and demand
— Always confirm commission rate before signing group contract
— Some suppliers pay commission on a net rate (you markup), others on gross rate
— International groups may have different commission structures
WHEN TO ASK FOR HIGHER COMMISSION
You may be able to negotiate higher commission for:
— Very large groups (50+ rooms)
— Multi-property or multi-year agreements
— Groups during low season
— Repeat clients
